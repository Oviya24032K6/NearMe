FORTUNE PARK

Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux, it spans 30 acres, offering a vast expanse of greenery amidst the urban jungle. Its picturesque landscapes, including the scenic Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis Reservoir and Bethesda Terrace, attract millions of visitors annually. Central Park serves as a cultural hub, hosting concerts, theatrical performances, and art installations throughout the year. The park's diverse attractions, from the Central Park Zoo to the Shakespeare Garden, cater to visitors of all ages and interests. Joggers, cyclists, and rollerbladers frequent its winding paths and trails, while families enjoy picnics and boat rides on the iconic Central Park Lake. Notable landmarks within the park include the Bethesda Fountain, Bow Bridge, and the iconic Central Park Carousel.
